5 Creative Ways to Use QR Codes for Your Small Business

Published on August 8, 2025

QR codes are more than just a link to your website. They are a powerful bridge between the physical and digital worlds. Here are five creative ways your small business can use them to engage customers and boost sales.

  • Link to a Video Tutorial: On product packaging, add a QR code that links to a video showing how to use or assemble the product. This adds immense value and reduces customer support queries.
  • Exclusive Discounts: Place a QR code on your flyers or in-store signage that offers a special discount. This rewards customers for their visit and encourages immediate purchases.
  • Gather Feedback: Put a QR code on receipts that links to a simple feedback form. Offer a small incentive, like 10% off their next purchase, for completing the survey.
  • Wi-Fi Access: If you run a café, restaurant, or any place with a waiting area, a QR code that grants instant Wi-Fi access is a huge convenience for your customers.
  • Interactive Business Cards: Go beyond a name and number. Add a QR code to your business card that links to your online portfolio, a personal introduction video, or your LinkedIn profile.

Static vs. Dynamic QR Codes: Which One Do You Need?

Published on August 8, 2025

Not all QR codes are created equal. Understanding the difference between static and dynamic QR codes is essential for choosing the right tool for your needs.

Static QR Codes

A static QR code has the destination data encoded directly into the pattern. This means once it's generated, the information cannot be changed. Our generator creates static QR codes.

  • Best for: Permanent information like your website URL, Wi-Fi passwords, or contact details on a business card.
  • Pros: They are free, never expire, and don't require any ongoing management.
  • Cons: You cannot edit the destination link or track scan analytics. If your website URL changes, you need to generate a new QR code.

Dynamic QR Codes

A dynamic QR code contains a short redirection URL that points to the final destination. This allows you to edit the destination link at any time without changing the QR code itself. These usually require a paid subscription service.

  • Best for: Marketing campaigns, event promotions, or any situation where the linked content might change.
  • Pros: You can update the destination URL, track the number of scans, see the location of scanners, and more.
  • Cons: They rely on a third-party service and often require a subscription fee to maintain functionality.

The bottom line: For most everyday uses, a free, reliable static QR code is all you need. For advanced marketing campaigns where tracking and flexibility are key, a dynamic QR code service is worth considering.

How to Add a QR Code to Your Wedding Invitations

Published on August 8, 2025

Make it easy for your guests to access all your wedding information by adding a simple, elegant QR code to your invitations. It's a modern touch that saves space and streamlines communication.

Step 1: Create a Central Hub

Before making the QR code, you need a single webpage to direct your guests to. This could be a free wedding website from services like The Knot or Zola, or even a simple, unlisted page on a personal blog. This page should contain all the key details:

  • Your full wedding day schedule.
  • A link to your online RSVP form (e.g., a Google Form).
  • Links to your gift registries.
  • Maps and directions to the venue(s).
  • Hotel and accommodation suggestions.

Step 2: Generate Your QR Code

Copy the URL of your wedding website. Paste it into the text field on our homepage and click "Generate QR Code".

Step 3: Download and Add to Your Design

Download the high-quality PNG of your QR code. You can then provide this file to your invitation designer or import it into your own design on platforms like Canva. Place it discreetly on the back of the invitation or on a separate details card. A small heading like "Scan for details" is all you need.

QR Codes for Restaurants: A Guide to Contactless Menus

Published on August 8, 2025

Contactless menus are here to stay. They are hygienic, easy to update, and cost-effective. Here’s how your restaurant can implement them in minutes.

Step 1: Create Your Digital Menu

First, you need a digital version of your menu. The simplest way is to create a well-formatted PDF. For a better mobile experience, create a simple, responsive webpage. This ensures the text is always easy to read on any device. You can host this page on your existing restaurant website.

Step 2: Generate a QR Code for Your Menu URL

Once your digital menu is live, copy its URL. Paste that link into our QR code generator to create your menu code.

Step 3: Print and Display

Download the QR code and print it. Place it in visible locations throughout your restaurant. Good options include:

  • On small, durable table tents for each table.
  • As a sticker on the corner of the table.
  • In a small frame at the host stand.

By using a QR code menu, you can easily update prices or add seasonal specials without having to reprint hundreds of physical menus, saving you time and money.

Are QR Codes Safe? How to Spot a Malicious Code

Published on August 8, 2025

While incredibly useful, QR codes can sometimes be used for malicious purposes, a practice known as "quishing" (QR phishing). Here’s how to stay safe.

How Scams Work

Scammers can place a sticker of a malicious QR code over a legitimate one in a public place, like on a parking meter or a restaurant menu. When you scan it, it might take you to a fake website that looks real and asks for your login credentials or payment information.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Check for Tampering: Before you scan a QR code in public, physically inspect it. Does it look like a sticker placed on top of another image? If so, be cautious.
  • Preview the URL: Most modern phone cameras will show you a preview of the URL before you open it. Read it carefully. Does it look like the official website? Watch for subtle misspellings (e.g., "PayPa1.com" instead of "PayPal.com").
  • Don't Download Apps: If a QR code immediately prompts you to download an app, be very suspicious. Only download apps from official app stores.
  • Never Enter Passwords After Scanning: Be wary of any QR code that takes you directly to a login page. It's safer to navigate to the website manually in your browser and log in there.

By being vigilant and questioning unexpected QR codes, you can safely enjoy the convenience they offer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the QR codes free to use?+

Yes, all QR codes generated on this site are completely free for both personal and commercial use.

Do the QR codes expire?+

No, the static QR codes you create here do not expire. They will work as long as the data they point to (like a website URL) is still active.

What information can I store in a QR code?+

You can store a wide variety of information, including website URLs, plain text, phone numbers, SMS messages, email addresses, contact information (vCard), and Wi-Fi network credentials.

How do I scan a QR code?+

Most modern smartphones can scan QR codes using their built-in camera app. Simply open the camera, point it at the QR code, and a notification will pop up with the link or information. If that doesn't work, you may need to download a free QR scanner app from your device's app store.
